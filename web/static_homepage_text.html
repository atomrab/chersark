Chersonesos ARK

This is the static, archival version of the interactive ARK database constructed for the joint Institute of Classical Archaeology-National Preserve of Tauric Chersonesos excavations in the South Region of Crimean Chersonesos between 2001 and 2006. The site replicates the form and content of the original, which will be located here until it can no longer be maintained, but does not include the dynamic search, display, and export functionality of the interactive version.

To compensate for the loss of that functionality, and to ensure the reusability of the data, a series of static exports are also available. A complete serialization in JSON-LD can be found here. Full exports in CSV format of the various data categories can be found here. The spatial data for the state plan, context extents and details, and small find locations in GeoJSON and shapefile format can be found here, and an interactive online map with the full spatial data from the project will be maintained here as long as it is possible to do so. All text, tabular, and vector data from the project have been also been deposited in the Texas Data Repository Dataverse for long-term preservation and access. The original image resources, including raster data for the site GIS and photographic sets used for digital photogrammetry and RTI, are currently held in an iRODS storage system at the Texas Advanced Computing Center.

Additional information about the project and context for this data can be found at ICA's website, in ICA's Annual Reports for 2001-2011, in our digital companion to ICA's work at Chersonesos, which houses the linked Wordpress site containing excavation diary entries for the 2004-2006 seasons, and in the final publication of the South Region excavations. Funding for the development of ARK in the context of the Chersonesos excavations was provided over many years through the generous support of the Packard Humanities Institute; funding for the final conversion of the South Region ARK to this archival format was provided by a grant from the Shelby White and Leon Levy Program for Archaeological Publications.
